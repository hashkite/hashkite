Frequently Asked Questions

Drupal is an open-source content management system (CMS) renowned for its flexibility, scalability, and robust features. It is ideal for creating websites of all sizes, from simple blogs to complex enterprise-level applications. Choosing Drupal gives you access to a vast library of modules for extending functionality, powerful tools for content management, and a highly active community for support and innovation. Additionally, it offers exceptional security and performance, making it a trusted choice for governments, enterprises, and educational institutions worldwide.

Drupal is suitable for businesses of all sizes. For small businesses, it provides a cost-effective and scalable platform that can grow with their needs. Its modular architecture allows you to start with essential features and add functionality as your business evolves. For large organizations, Drupal offers enterprise-grade performance, security, and the ability to handle high traffic and complex workflows. Whether you need a basic informational site or a sophisticated e-commerce platform, Drupal can be tailored to meet your requirements.

Drupal offers numerous benefits for website development, including:

  • Customization: Highly modular architecture allows you to create unique websites tailored to specific needs.
  • Scalability: Designed to handle everything from small sites to high-traffic platforms with ease.
  • Security: Trusted by governments and enterprises for its robust security features and active monitoring by the community.
  • Multilingual Support: Built-in capabilities for creating multilingual websites.
  • SEO-Friendly: Tools for optimizing your site for search engines are included.
  • Community: Backed by a large, active community providing continuous improvements and support.

These features make Drupal a versatile choice for businesses and organizations looking for long-term solutions.

A decoupled (or headless) Drupal website separates the backend (where content is created and stored) from the frontend (what users see). In this architecture, Drupal serves as a content repository, and modern JavaScript frameworks like React, Angular, or Vue.js are used for the frontend interface. This approach provides flexibility to build dynamic and interactive web experiences, especially useful for applications like progressive web apps (PWAs) or single-page applications (SPAs).

This makes Drupal an excellent choice for businesses targeting mobile users.
